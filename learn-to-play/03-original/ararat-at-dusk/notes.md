# Ararat at Dusk

**Composer:** Tony Koop / Heifer Zephyr (2025)
**License:** MIT — free to perform, record, arrange, and adapt
**Form:** Through-composed, 28 bars, ♩=54
**Instrument:** A duduk

## Story
Mount Ararat is visible from Yerevan but stands in Turkey — a presence that cannot be
reached. This piece sits in that quality: A Phrygian (the b2 is always there, unresolved),
the melody circling without arriving. Written for the duduk because no other instrument
holds longing the way a duduk does.

## Scale
A Phrygian: A Bb C D E F G. The Bb (half step above the root) is the instrument's
most characteristic interval. Use it deliberately.

## Practice tip
The long rests (`z4`) are part of the music. Do not fill them. Breathe during them,
but don't rush back in.
