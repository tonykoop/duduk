# Slide 1 - Duduk Family

L2 engineering packet for Armenian duduk and related mey, balaban, and duduki geometry.

# Slide 2 - Project Intent

Document a cautious shop prototype path for a reed-dependent heritage instrument while keeping empirical, cultural, and validation gaps explicit.

# Slide 3 - Cultural Scope

This packet covers engineering geometry only. It does not claim to reproduce Armenian reed-making, performance practice, or tonal tradition.

# Slide 4 - Governing Model

Stopped cylindrical pipe, `f = c/(4*L_eff)`, with reed effective length and open-end correction treated as empirical.

# Slide 5 - Primary Target

A3-root Armenian duduk body, 0.472 in bore ID, 0.748 in OD, approximate 13.25 in body length before reed calibration.

# Slide 6 - Packet Files

Design, BOM, sourcing, cut list, validation, assembly, drawing brief, SVG section, Wolfram starter, risks, resources, and site are present.

# Slide 7 - Manufacturing Plan

Turn and bore the body, fit reed socket, source multiple reeds, then tune body length and holes against the selected reed.

# Slide 8 - Validation

Validation centers on bore dimensions, reed fit, all-closed A3, every tone hole, reed stability, and cultural review before public release.

# Slide 9 - Risks

Top risks are reed effective length error, unverified hole positions, reed quality, and overclaiming cultural authenticity.

# Slide 10 - Sourceability

Apricot wood and duduk reeds require supplier verification. Current prices and availability are not checked.

# Slide 11 - Readiness

L2 private draft. Not public-ready until build photos, measured validation, and cultural review are complete.

# Slide 12 - Next Actions

Verify reed/wood sources, fit test reeds, build A3 body with trim allowance, populate validation.csv, then revisit CAD and public release.
